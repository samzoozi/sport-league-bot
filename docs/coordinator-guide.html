1. Getting started (one-time)

1
Add the bot to your group. Add @HangarSportBot to your Telegram group like any other member.
2
Run /setupgroup. Only a Telegram admin/owner of the group can do this. Run it with no arguments and tap a weekday from the buttons that appear (or type /setupgroup Monday to skip the buttons). This is the weekday your league plays on every week, and only needs to be done once, ever.
Who counts as an admin? The bot doesn't keep its own admin list — anyone Telegram considers an admin or owner of your group can run coordinator-only commands. There's nothing extra to configure.

2. The monthly cycle, at a glance

Every month follows the same rhythm:

1
Open signups with /newmonth.
2
Players join or decline via buttons on the card the bot posts.
3
Lock the squad with /finalize — this also charges everyone their share.
4
Games happen week to week. Anyone who can't make the next game can /skip, and the bot automatically offers their spot to the waitlist.
5
Repeat next month with a new /newmonth.
You can get ahead of schedule It's fine to open and finalize next month's squad while this month's games are still being played — the bot always knows which month's games are actually next, regardless of which was set up more recently.

3. Opening signups for a month

Run:

/newmonth <YYYY-MM> <total_cost> [skip-dates...]

For example, to open August 2026 with a $240 total court cost:

/newmonth 2026-08 240

The bot works out every date in that month that falls on your group's weekday and posts a signup card with ✅ Join and ❌ Decline buttons. Players tap one to sign themselves up — there's no cap during signup, so everyone who wants in can join; capacity is only enforced when you finalize (see below).

Skipping a specific date (e.g. a holiday)

If the court isn't available on one of the usual dates, list it after the cost. For example, if August's Mondays are the 3rd, 10th, 17th, 24th, and 31st, but the 3rd is a holiday:

/newmonth 2026-08 240 2026-08-03

This creates the month with only the remaining four Mondays as game dates.

A month key can only be used once You can't run /newmonth twice for the same YYYY-MM — if you need to start over on a month you haven't finalized yet, delete it first with /deletemonth.

4. Managing the squad before finalizing

Re-showing the signup card

If the original card scrolls out of view, run /squad to post a fresh, up-to-date copy.

Adding or removing someone yourself

Sometimes you need to add or remove a player directly rather than waiting for them to tap a button — e.g. someone tells you in person they want in. Two ways to point the bot at the right person:

  • Reply to one of their messages with /addplayer or /removeplayer.
  • Tag them by typing @ and picking their name from Telegram's own suggestion popup (not by typing out an @username yourself — that only works if they already have a public Telegram username and have talked to the bot before).

/addplayer works even for someone who has never interacted with the bot at all — it registers a placeholder profile for them automatically (they just won't have an e-transfer email on file until they run /setemail themselves).

Starting over

/deletemonth <YYYY-MM> removes a month's squad entirely — but only while it's still open (not yet finalized), since finalizing already charges people and deleting shouldn't silently undo that.

5. Finalizing the squad

When signups are done, lock it in:

/finalize [max_players]

This does three things at once:

  • Caps the squad at max_players (default 14) — if more people joined than that, whoever joined last is dropped from the squad (no waitlist placement; they'd need to join a specific game's waitlist manually once games start, via /waitlist).
  • Splits the total cost evenly across everyone who made the cut, and charges each of them that amount.
  • Posts an updated card showing the locked-in "🔒 FINALIZED" squad — the Join/Decline buttons disappear since the squad's set.
Finalizing is final Once finalized, players can no longer join or leave via the card, and you can't re-run /newmonth or /deletemonth for that month. If you need to adjust the squad or balances afterward, use /addplayer//removeplayer won't work anymore — handle corrections manually with /charge//credit (see "Managing money" below).

6. During the month: skips & the waitlist

Once a month is finalized, two player-facing flows kick in — both of them only ever apply to the next upcoming game, never the whole month:

A player skips a game

A squad member who can't make the next game runs /skip and confirms with a button. The bot then automatically:

  1. Checks that game's waitlist (people who ran /waitlist to be next in line).
  2. If someone's waiting, tags them in the group with Accept/Decline buttons.
  3. If they accept, the bot posts the dollar amount they owe and the original player's e-transfer email, so they can pay directly — you don't need to get involved.
  4. If they decline (or nobody was waiting), the bot either offers the spot to the next person in line or announces that the spot is open with nobody to fill it.

Checking who's playing

CommandWhat it shows
/nextgameThe next game's date and exactly who's confirmed to play (accounting for skips/replacements).
/gamesThe whole month's schedule, with a confirmed headcount per date.
Rolls over automatically The day after a game happens, "the next game" naturally becomes the following date — there's nothing you need to do to advance it.

7. Managing money

Every player has a running balance (negative = they owe money, positive = they're in credit).

CommandWhat it does
/charge @user <amount> <desc>Adds a one-off charge to one player (reply to their message or tag them).
/credit @user <amount> <desc>Adds a one-off credit to one player.
/chargeall <YYYY-MM> <amount> <desc>Charges the same flat amount to every player in that month's squad, in one shot.
/creditall <YYYY-MM> <amount> <desc>Credits the same flat amount to every player in that month's squad.
/paid @user <amount>Records that a player has paid you (e.g. by e-transfer) — moves their balance up.
/balancesShows everyone's current balance.

Some situations where these are handy:

  • A player e-transfers you their monthly share → /paid @them 75.
  • You need to correct a mistake from /finalize (e.g. the court actually cost less) → /creditall 2026-08 5 court cost correction.
  • Someone needs to be added to a finalized month's charges after the fact → /charge @them 75 August court share.

8. What players do themselves

You don't need to do these for anyone — but it's worth knowing what they do, since players will ask.

/setemail <email>Updates just their e-transfer email.
/emailsLists everyone's e-transfer email, for paying each other directly.
/balanceShows their own balance and recent transactions.
/skipSkips the next game and confirms with a button — the bot automatically offers the spot to the waitlist (only works once that month is finalized).

Registration only happens once A player's profile (email, name, balance) persists forever once they've registered — they only need to tap "Join" each month after that, not register again.

9. Multiple groups or topics

If your Telegram group uses Topics (the feature that splits a group into named sub-threads, e.g. "Monday Games" and "Wednesday Games"), each topic can run its own completely independent league — separate squads, waitlists, and balances.

To set this up, run /setupgroup separately inside each topic you want to act as its own league. Every other command (/newmonth, /finalize, etc.) then only ever affects the topic it's run in.

10. Good to know / FAQ

Can I bulk-register everyone in the group at once?

No — Telegram doesn't let bots see a group's full member list for privacy reasons. Registration happens per player (they run /register themselves), though you can register someone on their behalf via /addplayer even if they've never messaged the bot.

What if a player has no e-transfer email on file?

They can still join a squad and the waitlist just fine. It only becomes a problem if they're the one who needs to be paid — e.g. if they skip a game and someone replaces them, that replacement won't see an email to pay them until they run /setemail.

What happens if too many people join before I finalize?

Nothing during signup — join is uncapped on purpose. At /finalize, anyone past the cap (default 14, or whatever you pass as max_players) is dropped, prioritizing whoever joined earliest.

Can I edit the squad after finalizing?

Not via /addplayer//removeplayer — those are deliberately locked out once a month is finalized, since squad changes need matching balance changes to stay consistent. Use /charge//credit to make manual corrections instead.

Can a player skip more than just the next game?

Not in one step — /skip only ever targets the single next game. If they know they'll miss several games, they'd run /skip again once that date rolls over to become "next."
